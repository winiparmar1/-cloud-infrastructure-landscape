# Cloud Infrastructure Market — Overview

> Numbers updated Q1 2026. Sources: Synergy Research Group, Gartner, Flexera, IDC.

---

## Scale of the market

| Metric | Figure |
|---|---|
| Q4 2025 global cloud infra spend | $119 billion — in a single quarter |
| Full-year 2025 cloud revenue | $400B+ — first time crossing this threshold |
| YoY market growth | ~25% — and re-accelerating after a post-pandemic dip |
| Projected market size by 2029 | $1.76 trillion (CAGR 21.4%) |

This is not a maturing market. It is still in hypergrowth. Any enterprise that has not committed to a cloud strategy is not "waiting" — they are falling behind.

---

## Market share — Q4 2025

| Provider | Share | Revenue (FY2025 est.) | YoY Growth |
|---|---|---|---|
| AWS | 28% | ~$115B | 18% |
| Azure | 21% | ~$100B | 25% |
| Google Cloud | 14% | ~$48B | 28% |
| All others | 37% | — | — |

**The share story is not who's biggest — it's the direction of travel.**

AWS has declined from 33% in 2021 to 28% in Q4 2025. Azure and Google Cloud are both gaining. At current growth differentials, Azure could approach AWS revenue parity by 2028–2029. Google Cloud's Q4 2025 revenue alone grew 48% year-over-year.

---

## Enterprise adoption reality

- **89% of enterprises** use multi-cloud strategies — near universal at scale (Flexera 2026)
- **94% of enterprises** with 1,000+ employees have significant workloads in the cloud
- **52% of enterprise workloads** are now on public cloud, up from 32% in 2018 (McKinsey)
- **80% of organizations** use multiple public or private clouds (Virtana)
- The average enterprise runs **364 SaaS applications** — most running on top of cloud infrastructure

---

## What's driving re-acceleration

The cloud market slowed post-pandemic as enterprises rationalized spend. It is now re-accelerating for two reasons:

**1. AI-driven compute demand.** Generative AI workloads require significant cloud infrastructure — training, inference, and storage. GenAI-specific cloud services grew 140–180% in Q2 2025 (Synergy Research). The Big Three committed a combined ~$240 billion in capex in 2025 to keep up with demand.

**2. Digital transformation is not done.** Only ~20–30% of enterprises are fully utilizing cloud at scale. The remaining migration runway is enormous — and the tools to manage it (observability, security, FinOps, testing) grow with every workload that moves.

---

## The implication for sellers

Cloud growth is not abstract. Every new workload an enterprise moves to the cloud creates:
- A new governance and visibility gap
- A new security and compliance requirement
- A new set of tooling and SaaS needs

The cloud infrastructure market is the foundation on which every enterprise software sale is built. Understanding it makes you a better seller of anything sitting on top of it.
